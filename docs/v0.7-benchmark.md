# sheep-pose v0.7 — held-out benchmark with second clip

YOLO26n-pose, 2.5 M params, 100 ep / batch 8 / imgsz 640. Same architecture, same recipe. One additional training video (523 reviewed instances, 11 videos). v0.7 adds a second genuinely held-out clip (Test_Clip_Morning) to the benchmark protocol.

|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Training instances | 98 | 313 | 405 | 428 | 471 | **523** |
| Training videos | 3 | 6 | 8 | 9 | 10 | **11** |

## IMG_3651 held-out benchmark

Same clip, same window (frames 367–521, 5.17 s, track id=34, ROI `(1220, 80, 1560, 390)`).

### Detection

| | v0.4 | v0.5 | v0.7 |
|---|---|---|---|
| In-ROI detection (window, 155 frames) | 149 (96%) | 153 (99%) | 153 (99%) |

### Ear-angle residual σ

| | v0.4 | v0.5 | v0.7 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Left ear σ | 4.06° | 3.66° | 3.70° |
| Right ear σ | 4.09° | 4.30° | 4.46° |

v0.7 partially recovers from v0.6's left-ear regression (4.65° → 3.70°) while right ear settles at 4.46°. On this clip, v0.5 remains the best single-version performer on left ear (3.66°).

![Ear angle on a stationary held-out sheep (IMG_3651) — v0.2/v0.3/v0.4/v0.7](v0.7-ear-angle-chart.png)

A stationary sheep should produce a flat ear-angle trace; deviation from flat is the measurement noise floor. The v0.2→v0.4 improvement is large and visible; v0.7 overlaps v0.4, i.e. stability saturates after v0.4 rather than improving monotonically.

## Test_Clip_Morning — second held-out clip

A genuinely independent held-out clip captured under morning lighting with different flock arrangement. Never pushed to the labeler, never reviewed. 1920×1080, 986 frames, ~30 fps.

**Target window:** frames 742–892 (150 frames = 5.0 s), track id=215, a background tan sheep near the fence line. Head centroid movement: σ_cx = 47.6 px, σ_cy = 25.3 px. ROI: `(1234, 267, 1719, 538)`.

### Detection

| | v0.7 |
|---|---|
| In-ROI detection (window, 150 frames) | 148/150 (98.7%) |

### Ear-angle residual σ

| | v0.7 |
|---|---|
| Left ear σ | 2.39° |
| Right ear σ | 3.29° |
| **σ_avg** | **2.84°** |

σ_avg = 2.84° represents ~7.1% of the SPFES 40° classification band.

### Two-clip comparison

| Metric | v0.4 on IMG_3651 | v0.7 on Test_Clip_Morning |
|---|---|---|
| Window size | 155 frames (5.2 s) | 150 frames (5.0 s) |
| Target head size (approx.) | 234 × 169 px | 316 × 185 px |
| Target type | Foreground dominant | Background near fence |
| σ_avg | 4.08° | 2.84° |
| Reviewed instances | 405 | 523 |
| Training videos | 8 | 11 |

The lower σ_avg on Test_Clip_Morning is attributable to a larger apparent head size (~1.35× wider) and higher subject-background contrast in morning light — not to a model-version improvement. On the same clip, v0.4 achieves σ_avg = 2.61° (see `bench_report-Test_Clip_Morning-v02v03v04.json`), and v0.7 is 2.84°. The two-clip comparison demonstrates that held-out σ is a function of both model quality and clip characteristics.

## Multi-sheep flock monitor

v0.7 introduces simultaneous multi-animal ear-angle monitoring. The renderer tracks every sheep the model detects and produces a synchronized multi-lane chart — one live ear-angle trace per animal with SPFES reference bands.

Artifact: `sheep-yolo/artifacts/synced-lanes-6ewes-pro-Test_Clip_Morning.mp4` — six ewes tracked simultaneously on the held-out clip.
