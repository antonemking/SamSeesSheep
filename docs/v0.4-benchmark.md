## The clip — actually held out this time

`test-clips/IMG_3651.MOV`. Filmed Katahdin ewes, Middletown DE pasture. **Never
pushed to the labeling pod, never reviewed, NCC < 0.23 vs every training
video.** Not in v0.2's training set. Not in v0.3's. Not in v0.4's.

Best motionless target: bytetrack id=34, a ~234×169 px foreground head facing
camera at conf 0.6+. Window: frames **367–521** (5.17 s @ 30 fps). Centroid
std cx=30 cy=32 — not perfectly still (the target is moving with the group)
but the calmest 5 s segment with a big foreground head. ROI
`(1220, 80, 1560, 390)` padded ~half-head around the centroid range to
exclude neighbouring sheep.

## Headline — all three on the same unseen clip

| metric | v0.2 | v0.3 | v0.4 |  Δ v0.2→v0.4 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| any-detection (full clip, 933 frames) | 909 (97%) | 931 (99.8%) | **933 (100%)** | +24 frames |
| in-ROI detection (window, 155 frames) | 145 (94%) | 150 (97%) | 149 (96%) | +4 pp |
| **residual σ (mean, 5 kpts)** | **10.89 px** | 8.90 px | **7.70 px** | **−29%** |
| raw σ (mean, 5 kpts) | 49.44 px | 46.90 px | 46.60 px | flat |

**Residual σ** = std of (kpt − rolling-median-7-of-kpt). Strips slow head
drift; leaves the welfare-relevant frame-to-frame jitter.

## What this comparison actually shows

**Honest reading.** All three models detect the target sheep on ~94–97% of
window frames. The "47% → 99% detection rate" deltas from the v0.3 hero
post don't reappear here. That doesn't mean those numbers were wrong — it
means **`IMG_3651`'s target sheep is an easier detection problem** than the
crowded multi-sheep windows in `IMG_3583` / `IMG_3601`. The earlier doc
flagged this explicitly: those v0.3 hero clips were already in v0.3's
training set, and the v0.2 → v0.3 detection gap was partly a function of
v0.2 subject-hopping in crowded scenes, not pure model superiority.

What `IMG_3651` *does* show, on a clip none of the three has seen:

- **v0.4 fires on every frame** (100% any-detection, vs v0.2's 97%). Small
  delta but it crosses the "always there" threshold.
- **Keypoint jitter drops monotonically with training scale.** v0.2 → v0.3:
  −18%. v0.3 → v0.4: −13%. v0.2 → v0.4: −29%. Diminishing per-unit, but a
  real curve.
- **v0.4 on held-out (7.70 px) approaches v0.3's in-distribution numbers**
  on its own training clips (4.10–5.92 px). Going from "barely-out-of-domain
  scene degrades us by 1.8 px" is a defensible generalization claim that
  was unavailable from v0.3 alone.

What it does *not* show:

- A step-change like v0.2 → v0.3 had in the previous post. That was a
  dataset-scale story disguised as a detection-rate story. The real
  dataset-scale story is jitter, and the curve is flattening at v0.4.
- That ear-angle as a welfare signal is now usable. 7.70 px residual on a
  234 px head = ~3.3% of head size. That's good for many purposes; whether
  it's good enough to distinguish a within-animal EUP% delta under a
  documented stress event is the v0.5+ question.

## Why raw σ looks flat

| | v0.2 | v0.3 | v0.4 |
|---|---|---|---|
| raw σ (mean) | 49.44 px | 46.90 px | 46.60 px |

These look almost identical and that's expected: the window contains
~5 s of slow head sway, and raw σ measures total dispersion. The
sheep's actual head motion is ~45 px across the window for all three
models — that's a property of the sheep, not the network. Subtracting
the rolling median exposes the jitter component, which is where the
training-scale signal lives. See `bench_held_out.py` for the math.

## Honest caveats

- **One held-out clip.** Five-clip ablation would be more defensible.
  IMG_3651 is what's available; v0.5+ should add more.
- **Single dominant subject.** This window has one big slow sheep with a few
  walking neighbours. The crowded multi-sheep stress test from the v0.3
  hero post is not repeated here — those clips are in v0.3's training set.
- **5.17 s window.** Same protocol as v0.3 doc — a stationary 10 s window
  doesn't exist in this clip. Numbers would shift slightly with a longer
  window, but the v0.2/v0.3/v0.4 ranking shouldn't.
- **Same `conf=0.25` for all three.** v0.4 does not benefit from a tighter
  threshold. Raising it would hurt v0.2's detection further.
- **Residual σ uses rolling-median window=7.** Conservative — smaller
  windows make v0.2 look worse.
