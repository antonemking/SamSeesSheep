# sheep-pose v0.2 vs v0.3 — comparison benchmark

> **Correction (2026-05-12):** the original version of this report
> claimed `IMG_3583.MOV` and `IMG_3601.MOV` were held-out clips. They
> are not — they were uploaded and labeled during the v0.3 expansion
> session, and their 2-fps-sampled frames went into v0.3's training
> set as `c74474f9` and `0d1655d2`. They are still out-of-distribution
> for **v0.2**, but in-distribution for **v0.3**. The numbers below
> are correct; the framing was not. A clean held-out benchmark on a
> clip neither model has seen is the next item.

YOLO26n-pose, 2.5 M params, 100 ep / batch 8 / imgsz 640. Only
difference between v0.2 and v0.3: training-data scale + diversity.

| | v0.2 | v0.3 |
|---|---|---|
| Training instances | 98 | 313 |
| Training videos | 3 | 6 |
| Training video IDs | 6f689b79, 7e53dfab, d6873739 | + 00d25853, 0d1655d2, c74474f9 |
| val mAP50-95 (pose) | 0.479 | 0.643 |

Run on two test clips: `IMG_3583.MOV` (= `c74474f9`) and
`IMG_3601.MOV` (= `0d1655d2`). These clips are **in v0.3's training
set** and **not in v0.2's** — so the comparison reads as v0.2's
out-of-distribution behavior vs v0.3's in-distribution fit, not as a
controlled held-out comparison.

## Headline numbers — both clips agree

| metric | clip | v0.2 | v0.3 | delta |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| in-ROI detection rate | IMG_3583 | 71 / 151 (47 %) | 150 / 151 (99 %) | **+52 pp** |
| in-ROI detection rate | IMG_3601 | 101 / 121 (83 %) | 121 / 121 (100 %) | **+17 pp** |
| residual σ (mean, 5 kpts) | IMG_3583 | 39.86 px | 5.92 px | **−85 %** |
| residual σ (mean, 5 kpts) | IMG_3601 | 11.72 px | 4.10 px | **−65 %** |

Both clips show the same shape of result: v0.3 detects every (or
nearly every) frame, and the kpts it places are 5–9× more stable
than v0.2's. IMG_3583 is the harder clip (three sheep crowding,
v0.2 nearly halves its detection rate); IMG_3601 is cleaner
(single dominant subject, v0.2 already at 83 %) so the absolute σ
numbers are tighter, but the relative gap is similar.

**Residual σ** = std of (kpt − rolling-median-7-of-kpt). It strips
out slow head motion in the window and leaves frame-to-frame
jitter — the welfare-measurement-relevant signal. It's also the
only σ here that isn't biased by the detection-rate disparity.

## Per-keypoint residual σ (px)

### IMG_3583 — three crowded foreground sheep

| kpt | v0.2 (n=71) | v0.3 (n=150) | delta |
|---|---|---|---|
| nose | 64.15 | 6.16 | **−58.0** |
| L_ear_base | 34.22 | 3.97 | **−30.3** |
| R_ear_base | 30.16 | 4.93 | **−25.2** |
| L_ear_tip | 41.09 | 6.15 | **−34.9** |
| R_ear_tip | 29.67 | 8.40 | **−21.3** |
| **mean** | **39.86** | **5.92** | **−33.9** |

v0.2's nose wanders 64 px on a 260-px-wide head — i.e. across a
quarter of the head, frame to frame. v0.3's is 6 px = sub-feature.

### IMG_3601 — single-subject close-up

| kpt | v0.2 (n=101) | v0.3 (n=121) | delta |
|---|---|---|---|
| nose | 8.41 | 4.97 | −3.4 |
| L_ear_base | 14.72 | 3.63 | **−11.1** |
| R_ear_base | 9.37 | 2.36 | **−7.0** |
| L_ear_tip | 18.72 | 6.52 | **−12.2** |
| R_ear_tip | 7.39 | 3.00 | **−4.4** |
| **mean** | **11.72** | **4.10** | **−7.6** |

v0.2 already does OK on nose here (8 px) but is sloppy on ear tips
(19 px / 7 px split — likely confusing which ear is which). v0.3
locks all five kpts to a few-pixel jitter.

## Held-out test setups

| | IMG_3583 | IMG_3601 |
|---|---|---|
| resolution | 1920×1080 | 1920×1080 |
| duration | 31 s | 31 s |
| target track (v0.3 bytetrack) | id=2 | id=277 |
| head size (avg) | 260×235 px | 233×163 px |
| motionless window (frames) | 590-740 (5.0 s) | 755-875 (4.0 s) |
| ROI box (px) | (940,460)–(1230,770) | (894,147)–(1238,444) |
| centroid σ in window | 22 / 46 px | 44 / 32 px |

In both cases the ROI is tight — large enough to allow head sway,
small enough that adjacent sheep don't get sucked in. A more
generous ROI inflates v0.2's σ further via subject-hopping, which is
less informative than the within-subject jitter we measure here.

## Why raw σ is uninformative here

Raw σ on each model's own in-ROI frames:

| clip | v0.2 raw σ | v0.3 raw σ |
|---|---|---|
| IMG_3583 | 51.14 px | 49.79 px |
| IMG_3601 | 46.88 px | 52.77 px |

These look flat or even inverted. They aren't — they're dominated
by *slow sheep drift* over the window, which is the same for both
models (it's a property of the sheep, not the network). For v0.2,
slow drift + anatomical wandering both contribute; for v0.3,
almost entirely slow drift. After subtracting the slow drift via
rolling median, only the jitter component is left, and v0.3 is
5–9× cleaner per kpt. That's the residual σ.

## Sanity check vs val mAP

val mAP50-95 (pose) went **0.479 → 0.643**, a 34 % relative jump.
On the two test clips described above, the deltas were:
- **detection persistence**: 47 % → 99 % on the hard clip; 83 % → 100 % on the easy clip
- **kpt jitter** (residual σ): −85 % on the hard clip; −65 % on the easy clip
- **kpt anatomical correctness**: visible in stills — v0.2 wanders,
  v0.3 lands

These numbers are real but read with the caveat in the correction at
the top: the test clips are in v0.3's training distribution, so v0.3's
99 / 100 % detection and ~5 px residual σ are an in-distribution fit,
not held-out generalization. The true held-out test is pending.

## Honest caveats

- Same `conf=0.25` for both. v0.3 does **not** silently benefit from
  a higher threshold. Raising it would hurt v0.2's detection rate
  further.
- Motionless windows are 4–5 s, not 10 s. Neither test clip has a
  10-s perfectly-stationary sheep window — the sheep drift.
- ROIs are tight on purpose. Looser ROIs let v0.2 hop between
  adjacent sheep and inflate σ artificially.
- Residual σ uses a rolling-median window of 7 frames; smaller
  windows make v0.2 look *worse* (less of its slow drift gets
  smoothed away). The window=7 number is conservative.
